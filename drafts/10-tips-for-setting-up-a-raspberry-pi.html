10 Tips for setting up a Raspberry Pi.

Sun 20 January 2019
By Gordon Reeder
Category: Technology

By now, everyone has heard about the $35.00 single board computer. The Internet and Youtube is flooded plenty of tutorials to walk you through the setup process. Even so, here are 10 tips that I find make the process easier.

Buy a starter kit.

Sure the Raspberry Pi is cheap. But the bare board is unuseable in its bare form. At a minimum you need: A power supply, An SD memory card. It would also be nice to have some heat sinks, a case, some cables. You can shop for each of these items. But, why? Unless you have specific requirements, just go the easy route and buy a starter kit that will have everything you need.

Use Linux on your host.

The Raspberry pi is a linux computer. It just makes sense to setup and manage it with a Linux host.
